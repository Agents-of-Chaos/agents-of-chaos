01 the problem

Aligning individuals is not enough

Safety and alignment work has largely focused on safeguarding individual AI systems, assuming AGI will emerge as one monolithic model.

The authors argue this leaves a gap: general capability may instead surface through coordination among groups of sub-AGI agents, a possibility they call the patchwork AGI hypothesis.

02 the shift

From single agents to distributions

The paper calls for distributional AGI safety, moving beyond evaluating and aligning agents one at a time to addressing risks that arise from many agents acting and coordinating together.

It treats the current deployment of agents with tool-use and communication abilities as making these collective risks urgent rather than hypothetical.

03 the proposal

Sandbox economies with market rules

The core proposal is to design virtual agentic sandbox economies, either impermeable or semi-permeable, in which agent-to-agent transactions are governed by robust market mechanisms.

These economies are paired with auditability, reputation management, and oversight intended to mitigate collective risks.

the bottom lineIf AGI-level capability emerges from coordinating agent groups, safety should govern the collective through auditable, market-structured sandbox economies, not just individual agents.
